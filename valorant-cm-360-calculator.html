Valorant turn distance

Valorant cm/360 calculator.

Use this Valorant cm/360 calculator to convert DPI and Valorant sensitivity into a physical turn distance. It helps you tune aim by how far your mouse moves, not only by the in-game number.

Formula

AimScale uses this Valorant hip-fire baseline:

cm/360 = 360 / (DPI x Valorant sensitivity x 0.07) x 2.54

The result is a useful starting point for hip-fire aim. Scoped sensitivity and FOV can still affect how a setting feels.

How To Tune Valorant Sensitivity

  • If you over-flick or shake during small corrections, try a higher cm/360.
  • If angle clearing feels heavy or you run out of mousepad, try a lower cm/360.
  • Change sensitivity in small steps and keep DPI unchanged while testing.
  • Test the value in the range first, then confirm it in deathmatch or unrated games.
  • Adjust scoped sensitivity separately after hip-fire feels stable.

Common Questions

What does Valorant cm/360 measure?

It estimates how many centimeters your mouse moves for one full 360-degree turn in Valorant hip-fire.

Is cm/360 better than Valorant eDPI?

They answer different questions. eDPI compares Valorant settings inside Valorant, while cm/360 is better for physical feel and cross-game comparison.

Can I use the same cm/360 from CS2?

You can use it as a starting point, then tune after testing Valorant movement, weapons, and scoped settings.

What Valorant sensitivity is close to 50 cm/360 at 800 DPI?

About 0.327 at 800 DPI. Treat it as a starting value because comfort, mousepad size, and role can change the best setting.
